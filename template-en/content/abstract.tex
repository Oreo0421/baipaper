\begin{tucsimplesection}{Abstract}
Falls represent a leading cause of injury-related mortality among older adults, motivating the
need for continuous, automated fall detection in residential and clinical care settings.
Conventional RGB camera systems raise fundamental privacy concerns and degrade under low-light
conditions, while thermopile sensor arrays---such as the Heimann HTPA $60\times40$ employed in
the SensIR project---offer an inherently privacy-preserving alternative that captures thermal
body signatures at low spatial resolution without revealing identifiable visual features, and
operates robustly in complete darkness.

This thesis presents \textbf{RTMDetWithPose}, a unified end-to-end multi-task deep learning
framework for real-time fall detection from thermopile thermal infrared imagery. The system
jointly performs person detection, seven-keypoint body pose estimation, temporal fall
classification, and multi-object tracking within a single architecture. A lightweight
\textbf{MobileViT-S} backbone paired with a \textbf{CSPNeXtPAFPN} neck provides
parameter-efficient multi-scale feature extraction from the fused dual-sensor input of
approximately $114\times40$ pixels. An anchor-free \textbf{RTMDet} detection head localizes
persons, after which \textbf{RoIAlign} extracts per-person features consumed by a
\textbf{SimDR}-based pose head that decomposes 2D keypoint localization into two independent 1D
classification tasks---eliminating the quantization errors of 2D heatmap decoding and achieving
superior accuracy under extreme spatial resolution constraints. A dual-task \textbf{GRU} temporal
action classifier jointly performs four-class posture recognition and binary fall detection,
sharing a common temporal encoder so that posture understanding directly informs fall
discrimination. \textbf{ByteTrack} maintains consistent person identities across frames to supply
coherent keypoint sequences to the temporal head. The framework is trained with a two-stage
strategy that independently optimizes detection--pose and temporal action components, enabling
modular evaluation and replacement.

Experiments on the SensIR thermal infrared dataset demonstrate that SimDR achieves a COCO
Keypoint AP of \textbf{0.802}, outperforming 2D heatmap and direct regression baselines by
approximately 40\% relative. The complete system attains a fall detection F1 of \textbf{0.776} with \textbf{precision of 0.951} and a bounding-box mAP of \textbf{0.866},
while comprising only \textbf{9.3M parameters} and running at approximately \textbf{80~FPS} on a
desktop GPU---well above the 20~FPS target for edge deployment. These results establish the
viability of unified thermal infrared fall detection for privacy-preserving, real-time monitoring
in resource-constrained environments.

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} Fall Detection, Thermopile Thermal Infrared, Pose Estimation, SimDR, RTMDet
\end{tucsimplesection}
