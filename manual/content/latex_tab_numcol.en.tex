Tables with numerical material should be designed in such a way that numerical
data of a column are aligned with the decimal point. The package~\ctanref{siunitx}
provides the column type~\texttt{S} for this purpose. Numbers can simply be entered as
decimal numbers. The package analyses the input independently and makes the
correct alignment. Table~\ref{tab:latex:tab:numcol} shows a corresponding
application example and Listing~\ref{lst:latex:tab:numcol} the corresponding \LaTeX code.

\begin{table}
\centering
\langinput{code/latex_tab_numcol}
\caption{planets' orbital elements at epoch J2000.0}
\label{tab:latex:tab:numcol}
\end{table}

\lstinputlisting[language={[LaTeX]{TeX}},style=block,float,caption={table with aligned number columns},label={lst:latex:tab:numcol}]{code/latex_tab_numcol.en.tex}

The package~\ctanpkg{siunitx} is very flexible in terms of configuring the
alignment of the cell contents and the display of numerical values.
If you deal with columns of numbers in your work, it is worth taking a look at
the package manual. \ctanref{dcolumn} is an older package for aligning older
package for aligning numbers in tables.
