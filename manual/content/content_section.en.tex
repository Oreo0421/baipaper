The outline of the paper is done with the usual \LaTeX outline commands \verb+\chapter+,
\verb+\section+, \verb+\subsection+, etc. Compare also the Listings~\ref{lst:tpl:article}
to~\ref{lst:tpl:book}. The \texttt{*} variants are not numbered and do not appear in the
table of contents by default. Options for customising the table of contents
are discussed in section~\ref{sec:content:form:toclists}.

For the outline, the following sample scheme is useful. Depending on the type
of thesis, the points that do not apply are omitted.

\begin{compactitem}
\item Cover page
\item Dedication
\item Short paper
\item Terms of task
\item Table of contents
\item Further indexes
      (possible at the end of the paper, see below)
\item List of abbreviations, symbols and designations
\item \textbf{Body of text}
\item Appendices
\item Bibliography
\item Further indexes
      (alternative, if not already set at the beginning)
\item Glossary
\item Index
\item Acknowledgement
\item Declaration of Independence
\end{compactitem}
